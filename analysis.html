Analysis

All images are reconstructions and do not contain actual client data

My analytical process is to take a business problem, formulate it into a series of questions, tests those assertions , and then deliver insights that can be used to help solve those problems.

Cost Per Event

Created a process to view labor, parts, and freight at a work order level. This allowed for the comparison of work orders by geography, service type, model, customer, etc. Ultimately used for pricing, competitive analysis, and internal chargebacks.

There were many players involved with the building of this project, but I like to think it starts with a data-mart that I built to relate a lot of disparate attributes of work orders together. Previously all of this data had been housed in some 7+ different reports.

Capacity Plan

This tool was built to model workforce needs for 1,000+ technicians across North America. Essentially it takes historical volume and shows how much workforce was needed by city to handle that volume and compares to how much workforce the company actually had.

The final component calculates the headcount for future needs based on forecasted volume. Ultimately the tool is used by managers to make decisions on whether they need to delay hiring or ramp up staffing.

Standard P&L Package

This was designed as a standard package to cascade out to all managers with P&L responsibilities. This analysis seeked to help managers understand these questions about the financial performance of their business:

  • How is my year to date doing so far against my budget?
  • What kind of earnings gap do I have for the remainder of the year?
  • How does my current month compare to the budget, forecast, and the previous month?
  • What are the top variances that caused me to miss the budget/forecast?
  • How does our forecast change from one iteration to the next?
  • What is my track record of achieving forecast?

Location Analysis

This analysis was designed to help a client decide which locations were the best candidates for expansion. The methodology was to calculate a target customer density, economic growth indicators, and finally internal data on the client's existing capabilities in that area. This was all overlaid into Esri GIS to be able to explore the data visually.
